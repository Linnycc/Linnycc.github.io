Systemic Credit Risk Modelling — CDS Market Analysis Credit Risk · Quant Research
Credit term structure decomposition across 600+ firms · 10 maturities · contagion & tail risk during COVID-19 shock
Mar 2025 ▼

Overview

Analyzed a high-dimensional CDS spread panel — 600+ firms, 10 tenor maturities (1Y–30Y), 2015–2021 — to quantify how the COVID-19 shock restructured the credit risk surface: term shape convergence, cross-firm contagion, and tail co-movement. Applied the core toolkit of multivariate credit risk modelling.

Methods & Key Results

  • Regime Shift Test: Welch t-test confirmed 86.6% average CDS volatility surge (p = 8.62 × 10⁻⁵) — statistically establishing a structural break at COVID onset
  • Term Structure Decomposition: PCA + Factor Analysis revealed convergence of short- and long-tenor risk factors during the crisis — signature of systemic stress flattening the term structure
  • Risk Segmentation: K-means + GMM clustering showed a 51.5% surge in firms entering the high-risk tier during the shock
  • Tail Contagion Modelling: Copula models isolated joint tail dependencies across maturities, mapping channels through which credit stress propagated across the term structure
  • Spillover Detection: Granger causality found a 45% increase in strong inter-tenor directional links — direct evidence of systemic contagion dynamics
  • Network Analysis: Correlation network maps showed how inter-firm credit dependencies collapsed into denser high-risk clusters during the crisis

Significance

Demonstrates end-to-end credit risk analytics on real CDS market data — term structure decomposition, contagion quantification, and tail risk modelling — tools directly applicable to fixed income research and credit desk analytics.

Commodity Price Risk Forecasting — Cocoa Futures Econometric Research
Hybrid SARIMAX + IGARCH price-volatility framework · 21.7% RMSE reduction · 6.97% MAPE · climate signal extraction
Mar 2025 ▼

Overview

Engineered an integrated price-and-volatility forecasting framework for global cocoa futures, motivated by the 2022–2024 West Africa supply crisis that drove cocoa to 50-year price highs. The framework jointly models price level dynamics (SARIMAX) and persistent volatility risk (IGARCH), blended via EWMA for a 12-month forward risk-adjusted forecast. Climate variables and structural break detection are embedded as core model inputs.

Methodology

  • Structural Break Detection: Bai-Perron test pinpointed a regime shift in Feb 2023 coinciding with the West Africa supply collapse — enabling regime-conditional model specification
  • Climate Signal Extraction: GAM analysis identified a 3-month lagged Ghana temperature signal as a statistically significant supply-side predictor (p = 0.037)
  • STL Decomposition: Isolated trend, seasonal, and residual components across pre- and post-break regimes to improve model stationarity
  • Price Level Forecasting: SARIMAX model incorporating seasonal dynamics and temperature exogenous variable for 12-month forward projections
  • Volatility Risk Modelling: IGARCH captured non-mean-reverting (permanent) volatility persistence typical of commodity stress regimes
  • Integrated Risk Output: EWMA blending of price and volatility forecasts into a final risk-adjusted price path

Results

  • 21.7% RMSE reduction vs. ARIMAX baseline
  • 6.97% MAPE in out-of-sample validation

Immigrant Homeownership Gap — 25-Year Visualization Data Viz · Policy Analytics ↗ Live Demo
Interactive choropleth + filter dashboard · 6 Census waves (1996–2021) · 13 provinces · immigrant housing equity analysis
2025 ▼

Overview

Built an interactive decision-support dashboard enabling policymakers to track the homeownership gap between immigrants and non-immigrants across Canada's 13 provinces and territories, using six Census datasets spanning 25 years (1996–2021). Designed to directly support evaluation of housing equity programs and immigration policy effectiveness.

Design & Technical Decisions

  • Choropleth Map: RdYlGn diverging palette encodes gap direction — green (immigrants ahead), yellow (parity), red (immigrants behind) — for immediate spatial pattern recognition
  • Dual Metric Toggle: Absolute gap (percentage-point) vs. relative gap (% of baseline) for different policy framing needs
  • Filter Panel: Census year, ethnic group, immigration period, province highlight — enabling segmented policy evaluation
  • Summary Cards: Color-matched to map legend for instant quantitative context at any active filter state
  • Data Handling: Northern territories suppressed (gray) where Census cell sizes fall below 100 — preventing misleading inference from small samples

Results & Significance

Deployed as a fully interactive R Shiny application. Translates 25 years of Census microdata into a live policy tool for evaluating the immigrant housing gap under interventions like Provincial Nominee Programs and the 2017 Foreign Buyers Tax — bridging statistical analysis and public policy decision-making.
